Must-reads

On this page I collected a few resources you might find helpful in case you want to try some of the things I did. The page is called must-reads for simplicity and because I tend to learn best from books. But there are also links to YouTube videos here. I've read/watched/taken all of the books, videos or courses myself. None of the links are affiliate links.

General

These are books that don't fit into specific categories but enriched my life or blew my mind or were just too good not to share.

  • This one, of course: Refuse to Choose! by Barbara Sher. I also loved Live the Life you Love, which is for everyone, not just scanners. Then there's her Ted talk "Isolation is the dream-killer, not your attitude". It's on YouTube.
  • Fat Chance by Dr. Robert Lustig explains why sugar is so much worse for our bodies than we think. I changed my diet after reading it.
  • Charles C. Mann's 1491 is brilliant. Mann shares the latest theories for what the Americas looked like before Europeans invaded the continents and the consequences of that invasion. It's an absolute must-read for an aspiring researcher in ANY field because he shows how personal and political biases influence, even create, scientific "facts".

Author business

  • Turning Pro by Steven Pressfield is a great kick in the pants if you want to turn your art into a profession.

Mindset

  • I'm a big fan of Dana Wilde. She wrote the book Train Your Brain, has a podcast and a free newsletter I look forward to every day. Your best starting point, especially if you're considering starting your own business.
  • Mel Robbins made a YouTube video about reframing fear as excitement.
  • Emotional Intelligence by Daniel Goleman isn't really about mindset but one of the books I think everyone should read in their life. He explains just how powerful emotions are and why it's so important to practice emotional intelligence.
  • Heal your Body by Louise Hay is my constant companion. Whenever something's wrong in my body I use it to find out what's really wrong in my life so I can fix it.
  • The 10-Day Anxiety Detox Plan by Lewis David works! The book also includes access to audio files and I'm still occasionally listening to the hypnotherapy recording. It's so relaxing. Mindfulness for stress and Anxiety by Antonia Ryan (who read the hypnotherapy recording) was also helpful.

Financial Independence

  • The Psychology of Money by Morgan Housel is probably the best place to start your FI journey. The book is entertaining and the chapters are short and sweet.
  • In The Richest Man in Bablyon George S. Clason teaches you about finance through stories. Great if you prefer learning that way.
  • Full disclosure: Not at all a big fan of Robert T. Kiyosaki and do not recommend any other of his products and courses. Only Rich Dad Poor Dad and Rich Dad's Cashflow Quadrant (if you want to run your own business).
  • Quit like a Millionaire by Kristy Shen and Bryce Leung offers a "How to" guide to reach FIRE. It was written for North American readers so a lot of the steps they mention to no apply to the rest of the world, but might give you ideas on equivalents you might want to try in your country. If you're in your mid-thirties and don't work in a high-paying industry (like I did when I read the book) you might not want to start with this book because it can be a mit demoralizing.
