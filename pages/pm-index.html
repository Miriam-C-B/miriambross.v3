Writing

Becoming a writer has always been one of my dreams. Turns out I needed to find my language before I could get started. English is my language. While I could (and did) write decent academic papers in German, English is the language my stories need to be in. It's what my characters speak. Goethe would call it the language of my heart.

So once I got my PhD and practiced writing in English (really fast!), I was ready for phase two. Writing a novel. Writing is a life-long project, perfect for a scanner like me because every project is as different as I want it to be. Planet Myrthe is the fictional home of the fantasy stories and novels I write and self-publish under the pseudonym M.B. Ratel. You'll find the official homepage, including a couple of free stories, here.

At some point I will also write a thriller. One book at a time.

I practiced:

  • writing really fast
  • improving on my process
  • going slow
  • focusing on the sentence right in front of me
  • asking for feedback and accepting it gracefully

I learned:

  • how to write and edit a novel
  • about ProWritingAid, an editing tool I absolutely love!
  • how the traditional publishing industry works. Many thanks to Jane Friedman for her stellar work.
  • to think of one book as several products, how to self-publish and how to run an author business. Joanna Penn from The Creative Penn has my eternal gratitude.
  • how important copyright really is. Kristine Kathryn Rusch's blog/newsletter is a goldmine of information.
  • book cover design
  • I'm not a good alpha reader and critique groups are not for me
  • writing is easy, revising and editing is difficult and publishing is scary.
